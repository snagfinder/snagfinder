# SnagFinder

## Problem statement
- Currently there is no centralised database for Bunnings BBQs. This means you
  don't know if there will be a BBQ on at a particular Bunnings on the weekend.

## Purpose of the website
- Promote community BBQs held at Bunnings
- Audience - people seeking a snag

## Background Currently the information is static. This means that someone will
"submit a bbq" and this information will be put into the map manually. In the
future I would like to automate this better.

## Progress I have developed this basic website using Copilot and Google AI
Studio. I would like some feedback on the current functionality and any tips
for improvement. This is a bit of a hobby project for me and I fully appreciate
your time and experience to improve this.

## Issues (and what I may need help with)

- Should I make it mandatory for someone to sign in to add a BBQ? E.g. a Google
  single sign on
- Submit a BBQ - couldn't quite figure out how to get the button to send email
  directly to snagfinder@gmail.com
- What would be the most efficient verification step? E.g. sharing link to
  facebook post or / instagram post? Or asking for contact details
- Needs a 'report' button of some kind in case there is any inappropriate
  submissions (that's why it's important to have some kind of verification
  ....)
- Should there be an archive of previous bbqs?
- How to have a saturday/sunday toggle for the upcoming weekend in the map
- Zoom into cities could be better

Anyway - that's a few things that I have thought of so far, but would be good
to get a sense from you what the big ticket fixes should be.

## Next steps

Once the website is updated with any major issues, I would like to share the
concept on Reddit e.g. r/Australia, r/Bunnings to seek further feedback and
appetite for the website. To keep up the engagement of the website, I may
create an instagram page. Have also thought of having posts on the Snag Finder
page to promote the community organisations and their work. Plus also an annual
"Snag-a-thon" where people can try to go to as many BBQs in a day. All ideas at
this point.

Bottom line I don't intend to receive any monetary benefit from this website.
It was just an idea I had after taking a couple of international visitors to
try out the unique Australian experience. It would be cool if Bunnings made
their own proper database themselves and have it on their application.
